MEXICO CAMPUS
Overview

Don Honorio Ventura State University - Mexico (DHVSU - Mexico) is a public educational institution established in 1997 as an extension campus of what then known Don Honorio Ventura College of Arts and Trades (DHVCAT).The institution operated as an extension campus for two decades and was just officially converted into a satellite campus in 2008 as part of the DHVSU system.

The Mexico campus provides undergraduate programs in the fields of Teacher Education, Industrial Technology, Business Management, Information Technology, and Hotel and Restaurant Management. The campus also has a ladderized program in Industrial Technology, the only ladderized program offered among the satellite campuses in the system.
